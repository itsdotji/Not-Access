About Us

NOT ACCESS is a decentralized, strictly client-side Progressive Web Application designed for absolute security. Operating entirely without a backend or server, your data never leaves your device.

Officially Tested & Recommended Environment

This vault is developed, tested, and optimized primarily for Brave Browser, which serves as the recommended environment for the most reliable, secure, and consistent experience. While compatibility with other modern browsers may vary, Brave remains the officially recommended browser for full-feature integrity, privacy-focused operation, and optimal platform stability.

Terms & Conditions

By using NOT ACCESS, you agree that you are solely responsible for your Master Password, Recovery Key, and saved data. The software is provided "as is" with no cloud backup. Loss of device or keys equals permanent data loss.

Privacy Policy

Strict Zero-Data-Collection:NOT ACCESS collects absolutely no telemetry, logs, analytics, or user data. All operations occur strictly within your browser's local sandbox (localStorage/IndexedDB).

Content Policy

NOT ACCESS is a neutral tool. Users are responsible for the content (passwords, notes) stored within their local vault. Kimi Technologies holds no liability for user-generated offline data.

System Warning

This application is designed to be highly secure. Running it via an active internet connection introduces potential browser vulnerabilities. Please utilize the "Add to Home Screen" feature and run airplane mode or disable network connections while managing keys.

DEVELOPER'S ULTIMATE WARNING

Save yourMaster Keyand Emergency Recovery Key.Take a screenshot. Write them in a notebook. Save them in your password manager. Email them to yourself. Hide them under your mattress. Seal them inside a vault. Bury them in a treasure chest and draw a map if that's what it takes.

Because the moment you lose these keys, your future self will suddenly transform into a professional detective, therapist, hacker, philosopher, and part-time archaeologist trying to recover something that literally came with a giant warning label saying:"DON'T LOSE THIS."

And when you return asking, "Bro... is there any other way?" the answer will be the same legendary response echoed by developers throughout history:"If there was another way, we wouldn't have called it an Emergency Recovery Key."

So don't test your luck. The universe forgives. Your friends forgive. Even your ex might forgive. But cryptography? Cryptography looks you straight in the eye and says,"Skill issue."

Save the keys now. Because if you lose them later, the only backup you'll have left is your tears, your regrets, and a PhD in saying, "I should've listened."
